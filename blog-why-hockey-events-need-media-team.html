Why Your Hockey Tournament
Needs a Dedicated Media Team

By Slapstick Hockey · June 2026 · 6 min read

You've secured the rink time, locked in the referees, built the brackets, and sold the registration slots. Everything's in place for a great tournament. But if no one's filming it, streaming it, or posting about it while it happens — did it really happen?

That sounds dramatic, but it's true in a very practical way. The content that comes out of your event is the marketing for your next one. And if there's no content, you're starting from scratch every single year.

Here's what a professional media team actually does for a hockey event — beyond just pointing a camera at the ice.

What You Actually Get

When you book a professional hockey media team, you're not just getting someone to record the games. Here's what a full package looks like:

  • Live coverage — streaming the event in real time so families, fans, and anyone who couldn't make it can watch live
  • Recorded 4K 60fps footage — professional-grade video of your event that you own and can use however you want
  • Social media clips — short-form content cut for Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook that drives hundreds of thousands of views and keeps your event visible long after the final whistle
  • Sponsor integration — logo placement in stream graphics and overlays, brand mentions during live commentary, and product features woven naturally into content

That's not just coverage — that's a full content engine that works for your event for months.

The Numbers Speak for Themselves

Social media clips from our event coverage routinely reach hundreds of thousands of views. Our coverage of the NHL Alumni Outdoor Classic hit over 10 million views across platforms. That's not a viral fluke — that's what happens when you put professional media on a great event and distribute the content the right way.

For an event organizer, those numbers mean one thing: your tournament is being seen by hundreds of thousands of hockey fans who weren't there. Some of them will be next year.

The content outlives the event. The tournament might be over in a weekend, but the clips keep circulating for months. Every view is a potential registration for next year's bracket.

It Fills Your Bracket Next Year

The number one way hockey teams decide which tournaments to enter is word of mouth and social media. If teams see highlight clips from your event, the energy of the crowd, the quality of the ice, the legitimacy of the setup — they want in. If they see nothing, they have no reason to choose your tournament over the six others competing for their registration dollars that same weekend.

A media team turns a one-day event into weeks of content: highlight clips, player features, behind-the-scenes moments, game-winning goals. Every post is a recruitment tool for next year's field.

It Makes Your Event Look Legitimate

There's a difference between a tournament that has someone's dad filming from the bleachers and one that has a dedicated broadcast team, professional graphics, commentary, and a live stream. Both might be excellent hockey. But only one of them looks like it's worth traveling for.

For tournaments trying to attract teams from outside their region, this gap matters enormously. Teams making a weekend trip are spending money on hotels, gas, and entry fees. They want to know they're coming to something real. Professional media coverage signals that instantly.

It Gives Your Sponsors Something Tangible

If you're trying to attract sponsors, you need to offer them more than a logo on a banner seen by the 40 people in the stands. A media team changes the value proposition completely.

With professional event coverage, sponsors get logo placement in stream graphics, brand mentions during live commentary, and social media content that reaches a genuine hockey audience — with real view counts you can show them. That's a sponsorship package worth selling. A banner in a cold rink is not.

What to Look for When Hiring a Hockey Media Team

Not all videographers know hockey, and not all hockey people know video. Here's what separates a team that's done this before from someone figuring it out on-site:

  • They understand the sport. Knowing when to anticipate a play, where the puck is going, and how to capture the emotion of a game requires real hockey knowledge. You can't fake it with equipment.
  • They have rink experience. Glass glare, arena lighting, crowd noise, limited power access — a team that's been in rinks knows how to handle all of it before it becomes a problem.
  • They deliver content fast. Social media moves fast. Clips going up during and right after the event are worth ten times what gets posted two weeks later.
  • They have a reel. Watch their past event coverage. Does it look good? Does the energy match the game? Would you want to attend that event based on what you see?

The Bottom Line

A media team is one of the highest-ROI investments a tournament director can make, because the output — live coverage, 4K footage, social clips — keeps working for your event long after the final buzzer. The content you create at this year's tournament is what fills next year's bracket.
