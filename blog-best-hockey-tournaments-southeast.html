Best Amateur Hockey Tournaments
in the Southeast You Should Know About

By Slapstick Hockey · June 2026 · 8 min read

People outside the South underestimate how much hockey is happening down here. The arenas are smaller, the accents are thicker, and the fan sections are louder than anywhere we've been. From the Tennessee Valley to the Mississippi River, there's a legitimate hockey scene building in the Southeast — and some of the best amateur tournaments in the country are in the middle of it.

We've had cameras at most of these. Here's what we know about them firsthand.

Breakaway Hockey Tournaments

Breakaway is the standard for well-organized amateur hockey tournaments in the South. They run events across multiple cities throughout the year — we've covered their Huntsville stop (Rocket City Frozen Fury), their O'Fallon, Missouri event (Arch Madness), and their Louisville Summer Showdown, among others. What sets Breakaway apart is the consistency: registration process, rink quality, and on-ice officiating are reliably solid across all their stops.

Huntsville, AL · May

Breakaway Rocket City Frozen Fury

One of the better-organized weekend tournaments in Alabama. The Huntsville area has more hockey infrastructure than most people realize, and this event fills up fast across multiple divisions. If you're in the Tennessee Valley and looking for a competitive adult tournament, this one's worth the entry fee.

O'Fallon, MO · March

Breakaway Arch Madness

St. Louis is a hockey city — has been for decades — and this tournament reflects that. The competition level is noticeably higher here, and the rink facilities in the O'Fallon area are excellent. Teams from across the Midwest make the trip, which means you're playing against squads who treat their rec league like it's the playoffs.

Louisville, KY · August

Breakaway Louisville Summer Showdown

The August timing makes this a late-summer staple for teams in Kentucky, Indiana, and Ohio. Louisville's grown its hockey scene significantly over the last decade, and this tournament is a beneficiary of that. Good ice, competitive fields, and a city worth exploring if you make the trip.

Hotter Than Hell Summer Bash

Based in the St. Louis suburb of Chesterfield, Missouri, Hotter Than Hell is exactly what the name promises — a summer tournament played when it's almost too hot to think about ice hockey, which somehow makes it more fun. The event runs in late July and draws teams from across Missouri and into neighboring states. The organizers have built a real community around this one, and the regulars come back year after year.

Chesterfield, MO · July

Hotter Than Hell Summer Bash

Good vibes, competitive hockey, and the kind of crowd energy that makes a small tournament feel like a big deal. If you're a Missouri-based team looking for a summer tournament to get the group together before fall, this is the one to know.

The Huntsville Longest Game

This one's different from everything else on this list. The Huntsville Longest Game is exactly what it sounds like — a continuous hockey game played as long as humanly possible, benefiting a charitable cause. We've been there for both the 2024 and 2025 editions, and it's genuinely one of the most impressive things we've covered. Players rotate in shifts, the crowd builds over time, and by the end there's an energy in that rink that a normal tournament can't replicate.

If you're looking for an event to sponsor, cover, or participate in that has a story worth telling, the Huntsville Longest Game is it. The cause, the format, and the community around it all make it stand out.

All Veterans Day Game — Huntsville

Held each November in Huntsville, the All Veterans Day Game is a charity hockey event honoring military veterans. We've covered it in both 2024 and 2025. The atmosphere is genuinely moving — it's one of those events that reminds you what amateur hockey can be when it connects to something bigger than a bracket.

For sponsors and brands interested in military and community causes, this is one of the most meaningful events in the Southeast hockey calendar. The turnout has grown every year.

Huntsville Havoc Events

The Huntsville Havoc are the SPHL's most consistently entertaining team in terms of fan engagement, and their off-season events are no different. The All-Star Skills Competition in January 2026 was one of the most fun things we've ever had cameras at — the players are competitive but loose, and the in-building experience rivals events with five times the budget.

With Slapstick Hockey returning as the broadcast team for the Havoc's 2026-27 season, there will be more opportunities throughout the year to catch Havoc events — including the home opener in October. If you're within driving distance of Huntsville and haven't been to a Havoc game, fix that.

What Makes a Great Southeast Hockey Tournament

After covering events from Alabama to Colorado, here's what consistently separates the good tournaments from the great ones:

  • Communication. Teams want to know their schedule, their division, and their bracket — and they want to know it in advance. The organizers who do this well get repeat registrations.
  • Ice quality. Sounds obvious, but rink conditions vary wildly. Events at facilities that prioritize ice maintenance have better hockey and happier players.
  • Something beyond the bracket. The tournaments that build loyalty are the ones that feel like events — not just games. Music, vendors, engagement activities, coverage that captures the moment. People come back to experiences.
  • A reason to come back. The best tournaments grow their fields year over year because teams had a good time and told their friends. Media coverage is the best word-of-mouth multiplier you have.
